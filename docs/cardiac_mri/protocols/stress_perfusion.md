# Stress Perfusion Protocol

**Indications:** Known or suspected CAD, ischemia evaluation (alternative to nuclear), pre/post revascularization assessment.

**Stress agents:** Adenosine (140 mcg/kg/min × 4–6 min), Regadenoson (0.4 mg bolus), Dobutamine (10–40 mcg/kg/min — for bSSFP cine stress wall motion if vasodilator contraindicated)

---

## Contraindications to Vasodilator Stress

!!! warning "Contraindications"
    - Active bronchospasm / severe asthma
    - High-degree AV block (2° or 3°) without pacemaker
    - Systolic BP <90 mmHg
    - Dipyridamole within 24 h (potentiates adenosine)
    - Caffeine within 12 h (blocks adenosine receptors — **hold coffee day of scan**)

---

## Sequence Order

| # | Sequence | View | Notes |
|---|----------|------|-------|
| 1 | Localizers | 3-plane | |
| 2 | Dark blood HASTE | Axial | Morphology |
| 3 | Cine SSFP | 4CH, 2CH, 3CH, RVOT, SAX | Baseline function / wall motion |
| 4 | **Rest Perfusion** | 3 SAX (base/mid/apex) + 4CH | 0.075–0.1 mmol/kg Gd bolus at ~2 mL/s |
| 5 | Rest cines (repeat if wall motion question) | SAX | *Optional* |
| 6 | — | ***Begin stress agent infusion*** | Allow 2–4 min before stress imaging |
| 7 | **Stress Perfusion** | Same 3 SAX slices | At peak stress; same contrast dose |
| 8 | Wait 10–15 min | — | Gadolinium equilibrates |
| 9 | TI Scout | Mid-SA | |
| 10 | **LGE** | SAX stack + LAX | Scar assessment |

!!! note "Contrast Dose"
    Use half-dose (0.05–0.075 mmol/kg) per perfusion acquisition to avoid T2* saturation artifact. Some protocols use full dose (0.1) — be consistent with your institution.

## Perfusion Image Interpretation

### Normal vs. Abnormal

- **Normal:** Uniform signal uptake in myocardium following gadolinium bolus. LV myocardium brightens uniformly from endo to epi.
- **Perfusion defect:** Subendocardial or transmural dark region on first-pass images, corresponding to territory of stenosed vessel.
- **Dark rim artifact:** Thin (1 pixel) subendocardial dark rim — common, especially at base. Not a true defect if limited to 1 slice and no corresponding LGE.

### Coronary Territory

| Territory | Coronary Artery |
|-----------|----------------|
| Anterior, anteroseptal | LAD |
| Lateral, posterolateral | LCx |
| Inferior, inferoseptal | RCA (right dominant) |
| Inferior lateral | LCx (left dominant) |
| Septal perforators | LAD proximal |

### Ischemia vs. Scar

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| Stress defect + No LGE | Ischemia (viable, stunned, or hibernating myocardium) — candidate for revascularization |
| Stress defect + LGE (>50% transmural) | Fixed scar — revascularization unlikely to help |
| Stress defect + LGE (<50% transmural) | Mixed — some viable myocardium |
| No stress defect + No LGE | Normal — no significant ischemia or scar |
