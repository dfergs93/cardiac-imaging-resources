# TMVR Planning CT

CT is central to TMVR candidate selection and procedure planning, with **neo-LVOT area** serving as the gatekeeper measurement for procedural feasibility. For scan protocols, see the **[Protocol Manager](https://dfergs93.github.io/radiology-protocols/ct/cardiac)**.

!!! info "Acquisition"
    Retrospective ECG-gated full-cycle multiphase dataset required (typically 5–10% R-R intervals) to enable neo-LVOT assessment across cardiac phases and select the optimal sizing phase (end-systole for annulus; multiphase for neo-LVOT simulation).

---

## Mitral Annulus Sizing

The mitral annulus is **saddle-shaped and D-shaped** — measure in **end-systole** when the annulus is smallest and most reproducible.

| Measurement | How |
|-------------|-----|
| **Annular perimeter** | Trace the full hinge-point ring in systole using sagittal + coronal oblique planes |
| **Anteroposterior (AP) diameter** | Mid-point from anterior mitral valve leaflet (AMVL) to posterior mitral valve leaflet (PMVL) insertion |
| **Mediolateral (ML) diameter** | Intercommissural distance |
| **Annular area** | Planimetry in reformatted plane perpendicular to the annular ring |
| **Annular height** | Septo-aortic horn (SAo) to inferior ventricular (IVt) commissural plane |

!!! note "Saddle shape"
    The mitral annulus has two high points (anteroseptal and posterolateral) and two low points (intercommissural). Perimeter- and area-derived sizing are preferred; 2D diameters alone underestimate true dimensions.

## Neo-LVOT Assessment

!!! warning "Critical Threshold: ≤1.7 cm² = High Risk"
    **Neo-LVOT area** is the single most important CT-derived parameter for TMVR feasibility. A predicted neo-LVOT area ≤1.7 cm² (170 mm²) carries high risk for dynamic LVOT obstruction and hemodynamic compromise.

**Simulation method:**

1. Place a virtual device (cylinder matching prosthesis dimensions) at the annular plane on CT
2. Reformat at end-systole — the phase when the AMVL reaches maximal systolic anterior motion
3. Measure the residual triangular orifice bounded by: virtual device body, AMVL, and interventricular septum (IVS)
4. Report minimum neo-LVOT area

| Neo-LVOT Area | Risk | Considerations |
|---------------|------|----------------|
| >2.0 cm² | Lower risk | Proceed with standard planning |
| 1.7–2.0 cm² | Intermediate | Careful assessment; consider multiphase analysis |
| ≤1.7 cm² | High risk | Consider LAMPOON, alcohol septal ablation, or alternate therapy |

**Risk varies by scenario:**

| Clinical Scenario | Neo-LVOT Risk Profile |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Native TMVR | Variable; depends on AMVL length and IVS thickness |
| Valve-in-valve (prior surgical bioprosthesis) | Generally lower — stented frame constrains leaflets |
| Valve-in-ring (prior annuloplasty ring) | Higher — ring constrains annular dilatation, AMVL may be long |
| Valve-in-MAC (mitral annular calcification) | Often highest — calcium bulk displaces device anteriorly |

## Adjacent Structures

| Structure | What to Assess |
|-----------|----------------|
| Circumflex artery | Distance from annular plane; risk of compression by device |
| Aortic valve | Aortomitral continuity angle; anterior displacement risk |
| Left atrial appendage | Relationship to device; LAA closure feasibility |
| Pulmonary veins | Proximity to device landing zone |

---

## Transseptal Planning

| Feature | Target / Measurement |
|---------|---------------------|
| Fossa ovalis thickness | <4 mm for unobstructed transseptal puncture |
| Transseptal height above annular plane | 3.5–4.5 cm (device delivery trajectory) |
| Interatrial septum-to-annulus angle | Determines catheter trajectory and device alignment |
| Left atrial dimensions | Adequate chamber size for device delivery system |

---

## Fluoroscopic Projection Planning

CT-derived coplanar angle prediction: the plane perpendicular to the mitral annular plane defines the optimal fluoroscopic working view. Report LAO/RAO and cranial/caudal angulations.
