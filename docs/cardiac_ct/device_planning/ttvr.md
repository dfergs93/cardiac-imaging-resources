# TTVR Planning CT

CT planning for transcatheter tricuspid valve interventions is the **least standardized** of the three transcatheter valve domains. The tricuspid annulus is larger, more dynamic, more non-planar, and more anatomically variable than the mitral annulus, and **right-heart contrast opacification** presents unique protocol challenges. For scan protocols, see the **[Protocol Manager](https://dfergs93.github.io/radiology-protocols/ct/cardiac)**.

!!! info "Acquisition Requirements"
    ECG-triggered **multiphasic** dataset: reconstruct at **5–10% R-R intervals** with **≤1 mm slice thickness**. Right-heart–optimized contrast protocol required — standard left-heart protocols produce inadequate opacification of the right atrium, annulus, and subvalvular apparatus. Consider split-bolus or modified injection timing to simultaneously opacify right and left heart chambers.

---

## Tricuspid Annulus Sizing

The tricuspid annulus is **non-planar and highly dynamic** — dimensions vary substantially across the cardiac cycle. Measure in **end-systole** for sizing.

| Measurement | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| **Annular perimeter** | Primary sizing metric; trace full hinge-point ring in systole |
| **Anteroposterior (AP) diameter** | Typically 40–55 mm; largest anterior-posterior dimension |
| **Mediolateral (ML) diameter** | Intercommissural (septal-to-lateral) distance |
| **Annular area** | Planimetry in best-fit plane perpendicular to annular ring |
| **Annular height / non-planarity** | Saddle shape — measure deviation from flat plane |
| **Coaptation gap** | Distance between leaflet free edges in systole; relevant for edge-to-edge repair sizing |

## Adjacent Structures

Right-heart anatomy is critical for safe device delivery and anchoring.

| Structure | What to Assess |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Right coronary artery (RCA)** | Distance from annular plane — RCA runs in the AV groove adjacent to the tricuspid annulus; compression risk with annuloplasty devices and replacement prostheses |
| **Papillary muscles** | Position and number of subvalvular papillary muscles; relevant for device anchoring and chordal preservation |
| **Right atrium** | Dimensions and morphology; large RA common in severe TR |
| **Right ventricle** | RV volume (RVEDVi), wall thickness, systolic function; severely dilated RV may impair device anchoring |
| **Coronary sinus** | Position relative to annulus; relevant for annuloplasty ring and some replacement systems |
| **Atrioventricular node** | Septal location — conduction risk with device implantation near the septum |

## Vascular Access and Delivery Routes

| Access Route | Relevant Measurements | Notes |
|-------------|----------------------|-------|
| **Transfemoral (venous)** | IVC diameter; IVC-to-annulus distance; IVC angulation | Most common; large-bore venous sheaths (24–36 Fr) |
| **Transjugular** | Jugular-to-annulus distance; SVC-to-RA-to-annulus angle | Used for some annuloplasty and replacement systems |
| **Subclavian / axillary (venous)** | Vessel diameter; angulation to RA | Alternative venous access |
| **Transatrial / direct RA** | Surgical hybrid approach | Less common |

Measure IVC diameter at multiple levels; assess for IVC filter (may preclude femoral access); evaluate hepatic vein anatomy (relevant for caval valve implantation).

---

## Device Class–Specific Parameters

Different device classes require different CT measurements:

| Device Class | Key CT Parameters |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Edge-to-edge repair** (e.g., TRILUMINATE, CLASP) | Coaptation gap, leaflet length, annular dimensions, RV geometry |
| **Annuloplasty devices** | Annular perimeter, RCA proximity, coronary sinus position |
| **Orthotopic replacement** | Full annular sizing (perimeter, area, diameters), subvalvular anatomy, RV size, access route |
| **Caval valve implantation** (e.g., TRICENTO, CAVI) | IVC and SVC diameters at multiple levels; hepatic vein anatomy |

---

## Fluoroscopic Projection Planning

CT can predict the optimal coplanar fluoroscopic working angle perpendicular to the tricuspid annular plane. Report LAO/RAO and cranial/caudal angulations; the tricuspid annulus is typically viewed in a right anterior oblique projection.
