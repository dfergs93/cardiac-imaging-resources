# TAVR Planning CT

Pre-procedural CT is the **essential imaging modality** for TAVR candidate assessment. For scan protocols, see the **[Protocol Manager](https://dfergs93.github.io/radiology-protocols/ct/cardiac)**.

---

## Aortic Annulus Sizing

Measure perpendicular to the LVOT outflow axis (coronal oblique through the three hinge points of the aortic cusps). Measure in **systole** (or the phase with the largest annular area).

| Measurement | How | Notes |
|-------------|-----|-------|
| **Perimeter-derived D** | Trace annular perimeter → D = P/π | **Primary sizing metric** |
| **Area-derived D** | Trace annular area → D = 2√(A/π) | Cross-check |
| **Max / Min diameter** | Widest and narrowest annular diameters | Assess eccentricity |
| **Annular area** | Planimetry | Directly guides device selection |
| **LVOT D** (3–5 mm below annulus) | Perpendicular measurement | CoreValve sizing; LVOT obstruction risk |

!!! info "Sizing convention"
    Area- and perimeter-derived diameters are preferred over simple 2D max/min diameters for device selection. Eccentricity index = (max D − min D) / max D; values >0.10 indicate significant eccentricity.

---

## Aortic Root Measurements

| Level | What to Measure | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| Sinus of Valsalva | Max width across each cusp (L, R, N) | Must accommodate device; typically 27–35 mm |
| Sinotubular junction (STJ) | Diameter | Narrow STJ constrains self-expanding devices |
| Ascending aorta | Max diameter | Sheath compatibility; ≥45 mm = surgical threshold (bicuspid) |
| Aortoventricular angle | LVOT-to-aorta angulation | Affects paravalvular leak risk and optimal fluoroscopic projection |
| Coronary ostial heights | LCA and RCA from annular plane | See coronary occlusion risk below |

---

## Coronary Occlusion Risk

Measure coronary ostial height perpendicular to the annular plane.

| Coronary Ostial Height | Risk Level | Action |
|------------------------|------------|--------|
| >15 mm | Low | Standard TAVR planning |
| 10–15 mm | Moderate | Consider BASILICA; evaluate sinus width |
| <10 mm | High | BASILICA or alternative approach; Heart Team discussion |

Additional factors that increase risk regardless of height: narrow sinus of Valsalva (<30 mm), low STJ, bulky or fused leaflets, valve-in-valve procedures.

!!! warning "Bicuspid Aortic Valve + TAVR"
    Note raphe type: **Sievers 0** (no raphe), **Sievers 1** (1 raphe), **Sievers 2** (2 raphes). Eccentric or bulky calcification increases paravalvular leak and annular rupture risk. CT calcium scoring and geometric assessment (eccentricity index, calcium distribution) are essential. Coronary occlusion risk may be higher due to cusp fusion patterns.

## Vascular Access

| Access Site | Min Diameter | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| CFA (transfemoral) | ≥5.5–6.0 mm (device-dependent) | **Preferred route** — assess full iliofemoral runoff |
| Iliofemoral vessels | ≥5.5 mm | Assess tortuosity, calcification, occlusions |
| Subclavian / axillary | ≥6.0 mm (left preferred) | Alternative if femoral unfavorable |
| Transaortic | Direct ascending aorta access | Reserved for prohibitive femoral/subclavian anatomy |
| Transapical | Direct LV apex | Avoided when possible; only if all others unsuitable |

**Key measurements:** Minimum luminal diameter at each level; iliofemoral angle >90° = higher vascular complication risk; sheath outer diameter vs vessel diameter ratio.

---

## Fluoroscopic Projection Planning

CT can predict the optimal fluoroscopic working angle (coplanar view) by identifying the plane perpendicular to the aortic annulus. Report LAO/RAO and cranial/caudal angulations to the interventional team.

---

## Post-Procedural CT

CT is the reference standard for detecting **hypo-attenuating leaflet thickening (HALT)** and reduced leaflet motion (RELM) post-TAVR. Also essential for valve-in-valve planning (inner diameter of implanted prosthesis, risk of coronary obstruction).
