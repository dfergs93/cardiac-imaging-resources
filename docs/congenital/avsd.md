# Atrioventricular Septal Defect (AVSD)

## Anatomy

**Hallmark:** Deficiency of the atrioventricular septum resulting in:
- **Primum atrial septal defect:** Opening between atria
- **Inlet ventricular septal defect:** Opening between ventricles
- **Common atrioventricular valve:** Single valve tissue bridging both atria and ventricles

**Associated features:**
- **Left AV valve cleft:** Separation between left superior and inferior bridging leaflets
- **Goose-neck deformity:** Elongation and narrowing of left ventricular outflow tract (LVOT)
- **Anterior aortic position:** Relative to posteriorly positioned pulmonary artery

## Classification

| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Partial AVSD (Ostium primum ASD)** | Primum ASD only; individual AV valves present; cleft in MV common |
| **Intermediate AVSD** | Small inlet VSD; common AV valve tissue above & below |
| **Complete AVSD** | Large primum ASD + inlet VSD; single common AV valve |
| **Rastelli types** | A (symmetric), B (asymmetric), C (asymmetric with displacement) |

## Hemodynamics

**Left-to-right shunting:**
- Atrial level (primum ASD)
- Ventricular level (inlet VSD)
- Magnitude depends on defect size and relative AV valve insufficiency

**Associated pulmonary hypertension:**
- Common in complete AVSD if unrepaired
- Contributes to progressive left AV valve regurgitation

## CMR Assessment

### Morphology

- **Defect extent:** Dark blood imaging (HASTE/TSE) axial and coronal views delineate primum ASD and inlet VSD
- **Common AV valve assessment:** Identify single vs cleft valve
- **LVOT narrowing:** Sagittal views to assess goose-neck deformity and degree of left AV valve override

### Ventricular Function

- **LV volume and EF:** Secondary LV dilation from ASD and VSD
- **RV volume and EF:** RV dilation from atrial-level shunting
- **Septation:** Position of bridging leaflets at AV junction level

### AV Valve Regurgitation

- **Left AV valve:** Most commonly insufficient; quantify by 4D flow
- **Right AV valve:** May have cleft; assess competence
- **Severity scoring:** Mild, moderate, severe regurgitation

### Flow Assessment

- **4D flow:** Quantify shunt magnitude (Qp:Qs)
- **Bidirectional flow at defects:** If pulmonary hypertension present

## Associated Findings

- **Down syndrome:** Present in ~25% of patients with AVSD
- **Heterotaxy:** Disorders of situs may coexist (laterality issues)
- **Other congenital lesions:** TOF, hypoplastic left heart features may accompany

## Surgical Repair

### Indications

- **Significant shunting:** Qp:Qs >1.5 in partial AVSD, virtually all complete AVSD
- **Symptomatic patients:** Poor growth, recurrent infections
- **Pulmonary hypertension prevention:** Earlier repair if risk factors

### Technique

- Primary closure of primum ASD
- Closure of inlet VSD
- Reconstruction of cleft left AV valve
- Maintain separate AV valve orifices

## Post-repair Complications

- **Left AV valve regurgitation:** May progress despite surgical repair
  - Can necessitate valve replacement later in life
- **Subaortic stenosis:** Residual LVOT obstruction from goose-neck anatomy
- **Arrhythmias:** Heart block risk (proximity to conduction tissue)
- **AV valve insufficiency recurrence:** Valve leaflet degeneration

## Imaging Follow-up

Serial CMR and/or echocardiography to monitor:
- Ventricular volume and function
- Residual shunting
- Left AV valve regurgitation progression
- LVOT obstruction (if present)
- Arrhythmia development
