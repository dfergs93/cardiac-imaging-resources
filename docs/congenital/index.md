  <div class="chd-tab-content active" id="chd-tab-normal">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs</h3>
      <p>Situs solitus. Stomach bubble on left. Liver on right.</p>

      <h3>Cavae</h3>
      <p>SVC drains to right atrium. IVC drains to right atrium. Coronary sinus drains to right atrium.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Veins</h3>
      <p>Four pulmonary veins drain to left atrium.</p>

      <h3>Atria</h3>
      <p>Intact atrial septum. RA receives systemic venous return. LA receives pulmonary venous return.</p>

      <h3>AV Connection</h3>
      <p><strong>Concordant:</strong> RA→RV (tricuspid valve); LA→LV (mitral valve).</p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p>RV coarse, trabeculated apex. LV fine, smooth apex. Intact ventricular septum.</p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p><strong>Concordant:</strong> RV→PA; LV→Ao.</p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Normal origin and course. No anomalies.</p>

      <h3>Aorta</h3>
      <p>Normal. Ascending, arch, descending portions. Ductus arteriosus closed (ligamentum arteriosus).</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Main PA → branches into balanced LPA/RPA flow.</p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p>No defects. No regurgitation. Four pulmonary veins.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- TOF TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-tof">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs</h3>
      <p>Situs solitus.</p>

      <h3>Cavae / Pulmonary Veins / Atria / AV Connection</h3>
      <p>Normal.</p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">RV hypertrophy.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Ventricular septal defect (perimembranous or inlet).</span></p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Overriding aorta (25–50% override of VSD).</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">RVOTO (infundibular stenosis)</span>, typically hypoplastic PA.</p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Usually normal. Anomalous course rare but important.</p>

      <h3>Aorta</h3>
      <p>Normal (post-repair: assess for root dilation).</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Stenotic, hypoplastic PA.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Post-repair: pulmonary regurgitation (major complication)</span>, RPA stenosis.</p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p>Tetralogy = VSD + overriding Ao + RVOTO + RVH. Post-repair: monitor RV volumes, PR severity, RVOT gradient, Ao root dilation.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- TGA TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-tga">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs / Cavae / PV / Atria</h3>
      <p>Usually normal (situs solitus for D-TGA). ASD/PFO common for mixing.</p>

      <h3>AV Connection</h3>
      <p>Concordant (RA→RV, LA→LV).</p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p>RV and LV present. Post-arterial switch: LV systemic, RV pulmonary. Post-atrial switch: RV remains systemic.</p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Transposed great vessels: aorta anterior/rightward from PA (opposite of normal).</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Aorta arises from RV; PA from LV.</span></p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Post-arterial switch: re-implanted coronaries</span> (assess for ostial stenosis). <span class="seg-abnormal">Post-atrial switch: native coronaries from RV.</span></p>

      <h3>Aorta</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Post-arterial switch: neoaortic (native PA) root dilation common.</span> Measure annulus, sinuses, STJ.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Post-arterial switch: neopulmonary (native Ao) stenosis possible.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Post-atrial switch: assess for baffle obstruction or leak.</span></p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p>Discordant VA connection. Post-repair assessment: systemic ventricular function, coronary status, baffle integrity, arterial dilation.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- ASD TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-asd">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs / Cavae</h3>
      <p>Situs solitus. <span class="seg-abnormal">Sinus venosus ASD: defect at SVC or IVC junction.</span></p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Veins</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Sinus venosus: anomalous PV return (right upper often to SVC).</span> Other types: normal.</p>

      <h3>Atria</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Secundum ASD: fossa ovalis defect (central).</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Primum ASD: lower septum.</span> RA/LA enlargement.</p>

      <h3>AV Connection / Ventricles / VA Connection</h3>
      <p>Normally connected. <span class="seg-abnormal">RV dilation from volume overload.</span></p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries / Aorta</h3>
      <p>Normal.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Enlarged from increased flow. Balanced LPA/RPA. Qp:Qs >1.5 indicates hemodynamic significance.</p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p>ASD = atrial septal communication. Qp:Qs quantifies shunt. Screen for anomalous PV (sinus venosus type).</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- VSD TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-vsd">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs / Cavae / PV / Atria / AV Connection</h3>
      <p>Normal.</p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">VSD: hole in ventricular septum (location: perimembranous most common, also muscular, outlet, inlet).</span> LV/RV enlargement from shunting.</p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p>Concordant (RV→PA, LV→Ao). <span class="seg-abnormal">Outlet VSD: mild aortic override possible.</span></p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries / Aorta</h3>
      <p>Normal (or aortic regurgitation with outlet VSD).</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Enlarged from increased flow. <span class="seg-abnormal">If Eisenmenger (Qp:Qs <1.0): reversed shunt, fixed pulmonary hypertension, cyanosis.</span></p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p>VSD = left-to-right shunt through septal defect. Qp:Qs >2 = large, warrants closure. Eisenmenger = irreversible, contraindication to closure.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- FONTAN TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-fontan">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs</h3>
      <p>Variable (typically solitus but may vary with complex anatomy).</p>

      <h3>Cavae</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">SVC and IVC drain directly to PA (TCPC Fontan) — no RA connection.</span></p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Veins</h3>
      <p>Four PV (or anomalous return depending on underlying diagnosis) → common atrium.</p>

      <h3>Atria</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Single common atrium</span> receives PV return. <span class="seg-abnormal">RA absent or minimal.</span></p>

      <h3>AV Connection</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Single AV valve (from common atrium to single ventricle).</span> May be regurgitant.</p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Single morphologic ventricle (RV or LV type).</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">No subpulmonary ventricle.</span> Assess EF and indexed volume.</p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Single ventricle → aorta.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">PA receives passive flow from Fontan conduit (SVC+IVC).</span></p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries / Aorta</h3>
      <p>Variable. Aorta from single systemic ventricle.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">PA passive inflow from Fontan conduit.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Collaterals = total PA flow − Fontan flow (normal <20%).</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Fenestration: intentional R→L shunt, mild cyanosis.</span></p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p>No subpulmonary RV; passive pulmonary blood flow. Monitor: single ventricle EF, AV valve regurgitation, collateral flow, complications (arrhythmia, PLE, hepatic cirrhosis).</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- COA TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-coa">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs / Cavae / PV / Atria / AV Connection</h3>
      <p>Normal.</p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p>Normal (or LVH if severe narrowing).</p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p>Concordant. <span class="seg-abnormal">Aortic narrowing at isthmus (juxtaductal, distal to LSCA).</span></p>

      <h3>Coronary Arteries</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Bicuspid aortic valve in ~50%.</span></p>

      <h3>Aorta</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Coarctation shelf at isthmus.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Collateral vessels (intercostal, internal mammary) enlarged.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Ascending aorta and root often dilated.</span> Measure CoA diameter and velocity gradient.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Normal.</p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Post-repair: re-coarctation (10–20%)</span>, <span class="seg-abnormal">aneurysm at repair site (Dacron patches).</span> Aortic root dilation may progress.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- EBSTEIN TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-ebstein">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs / Cavae / PV</h3>
      <p>Situs solitus. <span class="seg-abnormal">Enlarged RA if severe TR.</span></p>

      <h3>Atria</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">RA markedly enlarged from TR.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">ASD/PFO in ~70%; allows R→L shunt if RA pressure elevated.</span></p>

      <h3>AV Connection</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Concordant but TV malformed: septal/posterior leaflets apically displaced ≥8 mm/m² from annulus.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Dysplastic, incompetent leaflets.</span></p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Atrialized RV: thin-walled portion between TV insertion and annulus, functions as RA extension.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Functional RV: smaller, often hypertrophic.</span> LV normal or secondary changes.</p>

      <h3>VA Connection / Coronary / Aorta</h3>
      <p>Concordant. Aortic root normal. Posterior displacement from enlarged RV.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Normal size/flow.</p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Ebstein: apical TV displacement, atrialized RV, universal TR.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">WPW ~25%.</span> Assess functional RV EF, ASD/PFO, R→L shunt. Complications: arrhythmia, RV dysfunction, cyanosis.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- AVSD TAB -->
  <div class="chd-tab-content" id="chd-tab-avsd">
    <div class="seg-analysis">
      <h3>Situs / Cavae / PV</h3>
      <p>Situs solitus. <span class="seg-abnormal">Associated with heterotaxy or Down syndrome (~25%).</span></p>

      <h3>Atria</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Primum ASD: defect in lower atrial septum.</span> RA/LA enlargement.</p>

      <h3>AV Connection</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Concordant but common AV valve</span> (bridging leaflets). <span class="seg-abnormal">Left AV valve cleft.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Rastelli A, B, C classification describes valve orientation.</span></p>

      <h3>Ventricles</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">Inlet VSD in AV canal region.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Goose-neck deformity: LVOT elongation/narrowing.</span> RV/LV enlargement.</p>

      <h3>VA Connection</h3>
      <p>Concordant. <span class="seg-abnormal">Aorta anteriorly displaced from goose-neck anatomy.</span></p>

      <h3>Coronary / Aorta</h3>
      <p>Normal sizing. Aorta anteriorly positioned.</p>

      <h3>Pulmonary Arteries</h3>
      <p>Enlarged from increased flow. Assess for PH if unrepaired.</p>

      <h3>Miscellaneous</h3>
      <p><span class="seg-abnormal">AVSD: primum ASD + inlet VSD + common AV valve.</span> <span class="seg-abnormal">Left AV valve regurgitation common pre- and post-repair.</span> Post-repair: residual MR, subaortic stenosis, heart block. Strong Down syndrome association.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

## General MRI Tips

!!! tip "General Approach"
    1. **Morphology first** — dark blood (HASTE/TSE) axial stack for situs, connections, great vessel relationships
    2. **Cine function** — SAX stack of each ventricle (measure volumes separately)
    3. **Flow** — 2D PC or 4D flow at Ao, PA, LPA, RPA, any shunt vessel
    4. **LGE** — assess fibrosis, especially in systemic RV patients
    5. **T1/T2 mapping** — if myocarditis, infiltrative, or congestive hepatopathy suspected

**Breath-holding:** Use free-breathing with respiratory navigator for uncooperative patients or complex anatomy requiring long sequences.

**Sedation/anesthesia:** Required for young children; coordinate with anesthesia for timing of sequences.
